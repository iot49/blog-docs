---
title: 'Couplers'
description: 'Automatic remote uncoupling of rolling stock'
---

N-scale trains usually come with [Rapido couplers](https://www.trains.com/mrr/beginners/ask-trains/understanding-n-scale-couplers/). They work fine - except for **automatic uncoupling** which for a few blessed modelers works perfectly and most is a source of endless frustration.

Several alternatives exist that are (of course) usually incompatible with each other, thus requiring upgrading all cars on the layout.

![Rapido Coupler](images/uncouppler/image.png 'Rapido coupler (n-scale)')

The coupler has a small pin at the bottom. Mechanical uncouplers lift this pin which in turns disengages the couplers. This requires very precise alignment of the cars over the uncoupler device, especially since in my experience uncoupling is possible only if one of the two couplers is lifted but not the other.

Some had success with attaching a [metal bar](https://www.1zu160.net/eigenbau/magnetisch-entkuppeln.php) or [magnet](https://jnsforum.com/community/topic/21011-magnet-uncoupler-for-arnoldrapido-n-scale-couplers/) to the rapido couple to push it up with a magnetic field to force release. I have not been able to determine how they solve the problem of pushing only one coupler up and not the other. Of course one could equip only one side of the train with such a device, but that would require the train to be positioned correctly over the uncoupler.

There are other creative solutions, e.g. from [Arnold](https://www.altemodellbahnen.de/t5115f144-Wer-kennt-die-Arnold-Rapido-Rangierkupplung.html) that may work in some situations.

A hopefully more reliable solution are magnetic [knuckle couplers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zU_Ak9FLSMQ) such as those offered by Kadee for HO and Microtrains for N which are quite common in the US model railroad scene and generally get good reviews. [Dapol](https://www.dapol.co.uk/collections/n-gauge-coupling) offers a [version for NEM 355](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U_ANUZZ7Xxs), making upgrading relatively simple.Unfortunately these do not match European prototypes. They are also rather [expensive](https://www.dm-toys.de/en/list/search/easi-fit/manufacturer/Dapol/spare-part/1.html), with possibly cheaper versions available from e.g. Ali Express.

![Dapol Coupler](images/uncouppler/image-2.png 'Dapol coupler (n-scale)')

A suitable magnetic field pushes the two engaged couplers sidewise apart, disengaging them. This works better than the mechanical solution and enables even delayed uncoupling. Permanent or electromagnets can be used for uncoupling.

So-called "digital couplers" e.g. from [Krois](https://krois-modell.at/de/Krois-Modell-Eigene-Produktion/Digitale-Kupplungen/Kupplungen-H0e-TT-und-N/Krois-Modell-N-Digitale-Standard-Kupplung-N-DS::8326.html) can disengage anywhere using a signal e.g. from a DCC controller and are typically installed in locomotives used for switching. Compatibility with knuckle couplers may be an issue.
